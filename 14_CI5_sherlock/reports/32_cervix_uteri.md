# Cervix uteri (code 32, C53)

## Quick take
The cancer that launched this project. CI5 captures the full arc: the clean Pap-era decline in the West; the unmatched Sub-Saharan burden; the East-Asian reversal where urban-China coastal cities have gone from very low to markedly rising (and this is what Shanghai's U-shape looks like). The UK's bimodal / early-peak age curves are a less-discussed fingerprint of HPV that CI5 makes very clear.

## What catches the eye

- **The classic U-shape in Singapore Indian women** — 40.4 (vol III) → 3.7 (vol X) → 4.2 (vol XII). 89 % fall, now flat. Same U in New Brunswick, Hawaii Japanese, Hawaii Chinese, Geneva, Hawaii White. The shape is a cervical-cytology story: crashing rates then plateau.
- **Uganda Kyadondo is the outlier where the rate kept *rising*.** 24.8 (vol I) → 54.3 (vol X) → 48.0 (vol XII). Tripled and hasn't meaningfully retreated.
- **Sub-Saharan African registries are still the world's top.** Zimbabwe Harare African women 86.7 (vol X) and 75.0 (vol XII); Zimbabwe Bulawayo 89.9 (vol XII); Malawi Blantyre 76.3. All 10–13× the world median. South Africa Eastern Cape 60.8. These registries carry the world's cervix burden in 2020s data.
- **Inland China is the most spectacular *rise*.** Jiashan County 1.18 (vol VIII) → 9.89 (vol XII), +738 %. Qidong 3.69 → 13.78 (U-shape). Harbin Nangang 3.03 → 11.24. Wuhan 3.87 → 11.03. Zhongshan 3.54 → 9.98. Beijing 2.30 → 6.15. All rising, and **none of them fit the global "screening-driven fall" narrative** — these are cities where screening is still being rolled out and the underlying HPV-infection cohort wave has yet to peak. This is the story that justifies the whole project and the HTML presentation.
- **Hawaii populations collapse together.** Hawaiian -92 %, Chinese -87 %, Japanese -88 %, Filipino -90 %, Pacific peoples -90 %. One of the cleanest "big-cohort screening" effects anywhere.
- **Early peak in UK women across all sub-registries, vol XI–XII.** Peak at age band 6 (25–34) in England, West Midlands, South West, East, South East — every large English registry. This is a striking early-age fingerprint that reflects the UK's earlier HPV-infection cohorts plus strong screening above 35 pushing the peak younger.
- **Bimodal age curves in the Netherlands and Denmark** — a second peak in the 70s. This "screening-leaves-a-tail" pattern is known but CI5 vol VIII–X shows it with large case counts.

## Caveats
- Hawaii populations have a pop_warning from vol VII; the magnitude of the collapse is credible but the exact trajectory inside volumes VI–VII should not be over-parsed.
- Rising inland-Chinese registries should be checked against HPV-prevalence surveys; a portion of the rise may be ascertainment as registries mature.

## Worth a deeper look
- **A Shanghai-plus-inland-China figure showing the coastal fall and inland rise** is the core story the HTML hinted at. Add Wuhan and Harbin, and it becomes a paper.
- The Sub-Saharan plateau at 50–90 ASR is a policy story, not a surprise — but CI5's 60-year window shows how long the excess has been sustained.
- UK's age-peak compression to age band 6 is a clear HPV-era fingerprint that deserves a methods note.
