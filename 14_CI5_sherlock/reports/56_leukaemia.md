# Leukaemia (code 56, C91–95)

## Quick take
Leukaemia in CI5 has one massive outlier — **Zimbabwe Bulawayo African women, vol II, ASR 42.5** — which is 8× the world median and stands well above every registry. Alongside that, the dataset shows clear Kuwaiti paediatric signals and remarkable stability in age distributions across decades.

## What catches the eye

- **Zimbabwe Bulawayo African women vol II: ASR 42.52** — extraordinary for 1960s-era African data. Almost certainly HIV-associated *before* HIV was named (lymphoid leukaemia / lymphoma overlap), but the magnitude is striking regardless. Sits as the #1 outlier for the site.
- **USA Hawaiian Hawaiian men vol I: ASR 22.99.** Third-highest outlier. Pacific-peoples haematological-cancer story continues to emerge across sites.
- **India Trivandrum men: the by-now-familiar U-shape.** 21.93 (vol VII) → 3.75 (vol VIII) → 5.46 (vol XII). Vol VII is real (it is the very highest Asian male leukaemia rate recorded); the dive to vol VIII is a scope change.
- **Kuwait non-Kuwaitis women vol IX: early-peak at age band 1 (0–4), ASR at peak 38.7** — paediatric leukaemia signal elevated by the large migrant-worker demographics (young population skew).
- **Bimodal age curves in India Tamil Nadu, Korea, Hong Kong, Chinese cities** — a clear paediatric peak (band 1–3) *and* an older-adult peak (band 14–17). This is the "two leukaemias" pattern (ALL in childhood + AML/CLL in late adulthood) showing up geographically wherever the registry is big enough to record both.
- **US ethnic contrasts 5× or less** — unusually small for a cancer site. Leukaemia is one of the more ethnically balanced cancers in CI5.
- **France Calvados men: U-shape recovery** 4.31 → 3.27 → 13.29. Pronounced rise, U-shape with a significant rebound.
- **Canadian NWT men sex ratio 18:1 (vol V)** is a small-number artefact — and one outlier nearly all early-Canadian-NWT data has in common.

## Caveats
- Leukaemia aggregates ALL + AML + CML + CLL — each with very different age patterns. Cross-registry comparison without sub-type split is limited.
- Zimbabwe Bulawayo vol II (1963–67) is based on small absolute numbers; the ASR of 42 is credible but the magnitude should be stated with wide uncertainty.

## Worth a deeper look
- The **Zimbabwe Bulawayo 1960s female leukaemia outlier** needs a historical retrospective — what was captured at Mpilo Hospital then, and is it really ALL/AML or a lymphoma-leukaemia misclassification?
- A CI5-wide "two-peak fingerprint" visualisation (paediatric + old-age) would show how well large registries can capture both sub-populations and how many smaller registries cannot.
- Kuwait non-Kuwaiti paediatric peak could be used to illustrate population-demography effects on crude age curves.
