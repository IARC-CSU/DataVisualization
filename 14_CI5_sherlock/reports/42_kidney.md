# Kidney etc. (code 42, C64–66, C68)

## Quick take
Kidney cancer is almost universally rising — but the rise is split between *detection* (incidental CT findings of small renal masses) and *real burden* (obesity, hypertension, smoking cohorts). Indigenous populations and a Naples-sized cluster of Italian men are carrying the highest current rates.

## What catches the eye

- **Canada NWT & Yukon women, vol IV: ASR 23.8 — 5.6× the world female median.** USA Alaska Native women 16.6 (vol XII), Montana American-Indian 15.9, Oklahoma American-Indian 14.5. The Indigenous excess is unusually prominent for this site and spans generations.
- **Japanese and US-Asian catch-ups: huge.** Miyagi men 1.13 → 12.46 (+1003 %); Osaka men 2.92 → 11.76 (U-shape); LA Filipino men 1.11 → 9.98 (+799 %); Singapore Chinese men 1.32 → 10.44 (+691 %). Much of this is incidental CT detection; some is real.
- **Shanghai men: classic U-shape.** 2.04 → 1.77 → 9.31. Characteristic urbanising-East-Asia pattern.
- **Spain Tarragona and Israel-Arab women: U-shape recoveries** — 1.54 → 1.32 → 4.82 and 1.26 → 0.79 → 3.40. Consistent with a late-arriving CT-detection wave.
- **Morocco Casablanca women and India Chennai women show bimodal age curves** with a secondary peak at age band 14 (65–69). The shapes are unusual for kidney cancer and deserve a sanity-check — could be Wilms-tumour vs renal-cell confusion in older volumes.
- **Women-heavy registries**: The Gambia 0.13 (M 0.14, F 1.04 — nearly 1:8 ratio, an exceptional inversion!), Barshi/Paranda 0.27, Northwest Territories + Yukon 0.36. Kidney cancer is usually 2:1 male:female; the Gambia's reversal is notable.
- **Ethnic contrast inside USA is 11–21× depending on volume.** US White-to-Asian ratios were 21× in vol IV, around 10× in vol X — narrowing but still large.

## Caveats
- **cancer_warning**: ICD-7 was used for the first three volumes, with some discrepancies versus later volumes. Don't read too much into the vol I–II → vol IV step.
- Incidental-imaging detection (late-1980s onward) accounts for a substantial part of the Western rise; corrected-for-stage analyses in national data would separate real from apparent.

## Worth a deeper look
- The Indigenous (Alaska, Canada NWT, Montana, Oklahoma) women's excess needs a focused review — the magnitude is striking and persistent.
- A "kidney + bladder as a single urinary-tract story in urbanising East Asia" paper with Shanghai, Miyagi, Osaka, Singapore, Seoul would land well.
- The bimodal Moroccan and Indian kidney age-curves could reveal a coding issue with Wilms tumour at the adult boundary.
