# Bladder (code 45, C67)

## Quick take
Bladder is one of the most definition-sensitive sites — CI5 flags that non-invasive tumours may be included, and several of the registry-level outliers almost certainly reflect Ta/Tis inclusion rather than biology. Setting that aside, the real stories are: (1) the **Naples cluster** of extreme male rates in vol XII, (2) the **bilharzial** (Mozambique, Malawi, Zimbabwe) women signal, and (3) the rising tide in Algeria and Israel Arabs.

## What catches the eye

- **Zimbabwe Bulawayo African women, vol II: ASR 34.14, 8.3× the world median.** A classic schistosomiasis signal. Mozambique Lourenço Marques women (13.53) also elevated. These are bilharzial squamous bladder cancers, not the urothelial cancer of smokers.
- **Southern-Italian "Naples cluster" in vol XII**: Naples Centre 42.2, Naples North 41.6, Naples South 41.5, Caserta 39.7. All around 3.8× the world median, all clustered. Navarra (Spain) 39.2 matches. Either tobacco-cohort persistence or industrial exposure — worth a separate look.
- **Algeria Sétif women, inverted-U**: 0.07 → 2.50 (vol X) → 1.14. Israel Arab women 0.22 → 3.18 (vol XI). +1500 % and +1100 % — the fastest-rising female bladder rates in the dataset, though from very low baselines.
- **Japan Miyagi and Osaka men: U-shape recovery.** 6.12 → 5.48 → 15.61; 7.50 → 7.03 → 17.54. Either delayed tobacco-cohort signal or better endoscopic detection.
- **India Trivandrum men: 17.63 (vol VII) → 1.94 (vol VIII) → 4.94 (vol XII).** Same vol VII–VIII cliff as seen in multiple other sites in this registry — scope change, not biology.
- **Italy Ragusa vol V: M:F ratio 24:1**, Karunagappally 21:1, Hungary County Vas 18:1. Mediterranean male-smoking signatures.
- **Kidney (code 42) and bladder both show a Miyagi / Osaka / LA-Filipino cluster of U-shapes.** Might be worth treating these two sites as a coordinated "urinary tract in urbanising East Asia" pair.
- **USA ethnic contrast persistent at 14×** in vol X (White 8.25 vs other 0.58 women).

## Caveats
- **cancer_warning**: bladder may include non-invasive tumours. Any cross-registry level comparison must acknowledge this — registries with good urology services count Ta/Tis while others don't. The Italian Naples + Navarra cluster may partly reflect aggressive TURBT + Ta capture.
- India Trivandrum vol VII–VIII is almost certainly a registry scope change.
- Zimbabwe Bulawayo vol II female rate is based on small numbers from a single 1960s volume — credible but cannot be tracked.

## Worth a deeper look
- **Naples bladder cluster in vol XII** deserves a standalone look: is it industrial (tanneries, dye), tobacco, or registration?
- **Schistosomiasis bladder cancer geography**: Zimbabwe + Mozambique + Malawi in the 1960s–70s, paired with Egypt Gharbiah — though Egypt sits lower than expected in CI5, suggesting under-ascertainment.
- The Algerian / Arab-Israeli women's rise is subtle but consistent.
