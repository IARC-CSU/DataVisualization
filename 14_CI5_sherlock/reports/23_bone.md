# Bone (code 23, C40–41)

## Quick take
Bone cancer has small absolute rates (median around 1) and the signal-to-noise is poor, but a few things stand out: the *Israel Sephardic women* bump, a consistent high level in some Italian provinces, and a bimodal age curve in large USA registries that almost certainly reflects a coding drift (chondrosarcoma / multiple myeloma boundary).

## What catches the eye

- **Israel Jews born in Africa / Asia women vol V: ASR 6.49, 8.8× the world median.** The top spatial outlier for this site by a wide margin. Small N but sustained — worth a look at the underlying sub-type mix.
- **Hawaii Chinese men peaked at 6.38 in vol VI, down to 0.00 in vol XII.** Dramatic inverted-U, likely driven by a handful of sarcomas — but a clean shape.
- **Italian provinces carry a persistent male-heavy signal.** Ferrara 17:1 M:F (vol VIII), Sassari 13:1 (vol XII), Barletta 10:1. Global M:F for bone sits near 1.3:1. Either genuinely male-dominant sarcomas in these provinces or a coding habit.
- **USA aggregate (SEER + NPCR) male age curves are bimodal** with the peak at age band 18 (85+). The low-age (adolescent) hump is visible but small relative to the chondrosarcoma/osteosarcoma-in-older-adults bulk. The USA bimodal pattern across vol X–XII may reflect how chondrosarcomas of the pelvis are being counted.
- **India Karunagappally women: +428 % (0.21 → 1.11) in a U-shape.** Plausibly better ascertainment through a local project rather than biology.
- **The USA Black men bone rate ran 10× the USA "other" ethnic group in vol I** — historical gap that has since narrowed dramatically.
- **Chinese Xiangfu (Kaifeng) men vol XII: 5.88** — 5× median. Along with small-intestine, this same registry tops outlier lists; suggests a local registration/coding peculiarity worth a phone call.

## Caveats
- Small numerators almost everywhere; rank bone findings by number of cases, not percentage change.
- The interpretation of "bone cancer" in CI5 mixes osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma, Ewing, and (historically, in some registries) multiple myeloma — sub-type splits would be needed for a real writeup.
- The bimodal US age curves are likely a registration artefact, not a biological signal.

## Worth a deeper look
- Is the Israel-Sephardic women bump real and persistent, or a vol-V artefact? Cross-check with other Middle Eastern registries of the period.
- The Italian Ferrara/Sassari male excess could reflect occupational (asbestos, chemicals) or counting conventions — worth a targeted ask.
- Consider running all sarcoma-containing sites together (bone + soft-tissue under "other") to separate real geographic signal from coding drift.
