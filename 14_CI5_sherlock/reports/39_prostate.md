# Prostate (code 39, C61)

## Quick take
Prostate is the PSA-era cancer: the vol VI → X inverted-U in every Western registry is essentially a screening wave. The deeper stories are (1) the persistent US Black male excess (ratios of 8–14×, barely narrowed since vol IV), (2) the catch-up in East-Asian men once PSA arrived, and (3) a clean Algerian U-shape that argues for pure ascertainment change.

## What catches the eye

- **US Black men sit at the top of every volume.** Peak prostate ASRs: Detroit Black 202.0 (vol VIII), SEER Black 185.4 (vol VIII), Atlanta Black 181.5, Connecticut Black 181.4, LA Black 176.3. The vol-X Black peak (Atlanta 206.7) is the highest site-specific ASR for any cancer in US data.
- **Asian catch-up is dramatic.** Singapore Chinese men 0.81 → 33.81 (+4074 %), Shanghai 1.32 → 16.77, Osaka 4.55 → 40.50 (+790 %), Miyagi 5.41 → 39.95. Half the rise is PSA uptake; half is obesity + diet.
- **Newfoundland and Labrador men: inverted-U from 20 (vol I) to 95.5 (vol XI) and back to 75.8 (vol XII).** Canada's prostate wave in miniature.
- **Spain and Israel-Arab men show the same inverted-U.** Granada 10.89 → 61.37 → 52.87; Murcia 15.81 → 73.35 → 68.29; Israel Arabs 4.32 → 34.13 → 24.37.
- **Algeria Sétif U-shape: 0.28 (vol VI) → 0.20 (vol VII) → 7.74 (vol XI).** Pure ascertainment pattern — the early volumes missed cases, then an active registry + PSA filled in the gap.
- **Uganda Kyadondo: 5.74 → 53.0 (+823 %).** African rise across the full CI5 window; not a screening artefact because PSA is much less prevalent. This is a genuine burden increase, plausibly driven by ageing population + westernising diet.
- **US Black-to-White/Asian ratios 8–14× across volumes** and barely narrowing by vol XII (Black 149 vs Hispanic 15 = 9.7×). One of the most persistent ethnic gradients in oncology.

## Caveats
- PSA screening entered clinical practice around 1987 (US) and over the early 1990s in Europe; any volume VII–IX rise in Western registries is at least partly attributable to prevalence-pool detection rather than underlying incidence.
- No formal CI5 warning, but the direction of the "inverted-U" peaks aligns tightly with registry-specific PSA-screening uptake timing.

## Worth a deeper look
- The Black male prostate excess across five decades is one of the most important persistent health disparities in US cancer data — a long-window CI5 chart would reframe it.
- Uganda's rise (+823 %) alongside other African registries (Zimbabwe Harare, South Africa) is a genuine non-screening story that deserves its own paper.
- Contrast Japan's pre-PSA (vol I–V) low rates, the post-PSA climb (VI–X), and the partial plateau (XI–XII) to reveal the "true" underlying incidence.
