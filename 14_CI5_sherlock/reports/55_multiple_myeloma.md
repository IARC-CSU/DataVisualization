# Multiple myeloma (code 55, C88 + C90)

## Quick take
Multiple myeloma has the largest persistent US Black excess of any haematological cancer — Black ASR sits at 2–4× the US White rate across every volume from III to XII. The signal is stable, not narrowing. Elsewhere the story is a universal low-level rise, largely driven by better serum-electrophoresis diagnosis.

## What catches the eye

- **USA San Francisco Bay Black men, vol III: ASR 11.64, 5× the world median.** Nebraska Black women vol XII 12.19 (4.3×). New Zealand Māori men vol II 8.82. Multiple myeloma's Black-population excess shows up as early as vol III and is still at the top of US-male rankings in vol XII.
- **USA ethnic contrast inside the same country reaches 26× in vol III** (SF Bay Black 11.64 vs Hispanic 0.44) — narrower but still 12–17× in vol X.
- **New Zealand Pacific peoples women: inverted-U 2.10 → 8.90 (vol XI) → 8.34.** A Pacific-peoples myeloma excess that has not received much attention.
- **The "universal rise" is the cleanest haematological trend in CI5.** Virtually no registry shows a decline; most are up 2–6×. Japan Miyagi women +3125 %, Singapore Chinese women +2000 %, Algeria Sétif men +1356 %, Colombia Cali men +1239 %.
- **Uganda Kyadondo men 0.66 → 2.72, U-shape.** African rise visible on top of the universal one.
- **Hawaiian Hawaiian women vol I: 5.99 (4.5×)** — a very early high reading for Indigenous Pacific that matches the Māori / Pacific-peoples signal above.
- **No cancer_warning, no bimodal or early-peak anomalies** — myeloma's age distribution is a clean unimodal older-adult peak everywhere.

## Caveats
- Much of the universal rise reflects better serum electrophoresis and bone-marrow biopsy rates over time. Some is real.
- Small numbers in early volumes for non-US registries exaggerate % changes.

## Worth a deeper look
- **The persistent US Black × myeloma excess is arguably the most important persistent racial disparity in haematology, and CI5 XII shows it still at 12–15× — worth a focused paper.**
- **The Pacific-peoples (Hawaiian, NZ Māori, NZ Pacific) myeloma excess** is under-described and could be packaged as a coherent indigenous-Pacific story.
- Decompose the worldwide rise into a "diagnostic access" component (pre-vol-VIII) and a "real rise" component (post-vol-VIII) by fitting piecewise trends.
