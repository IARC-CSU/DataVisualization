# Other endocrine (code 51, C74–75)

## Quick take
C74–75 covers adrenal + pituitary + pineal. Very rare. The striking finding is Sweden: Swedish women, vols III–VI, run ASRs of 3.5–6.6 per 100 000 — 8–20× the world median. No other registry comes close. Almost certainly a registration practice (probably neuroblastoma + adrenal cortical tumour capture) rather than biology.

## What catches the eye

- **Sweden women, vol VI: ASR 6.56, 20× the world median.** Vol IV 6.25 (18×); vol V 5.60 (16×); vol III 3.56 (9×). Swedish men in the same era 2.86–3.83 (8×). By vol VII the Swedish rates settle closer to the rest of Europe — suggesting a coding/aggregation change.
- **Italy Trieste women, vol VII: ASR 3.13, 8× median.** Another small pocket of historical excess.
- **Early-age peak at age band 1 (0–4) in virtually every large US sample** — this is neuroblastoma showing up clearly in the data, even under the "other endocrine" aggregate.
- **Rising trends from very low baselines**: Poland Cracow +4200 % women, +2400 % men; China Tianjin +2520 % women; France Isère, Slovenia inverted-U's.
- **Bimodal age curves in US aggregate data** — the paediatric (neuroblastoma) peak + older adrenal peak visible in large enough samples.
- **Ethnic / sex-ratio extremes are small-number noise.** Spain Girona 67:1 M:F is based on single-digit counts.

## Caveats
- Sweden's elevated vol III–VI rates may reflect inclusion of neuroblastoma under C74 before reclassification — critical to verify before writing up.
- Rates everywhere else are low enough that single-case swings drive the rankings.

## Worth a deeper look
- **Sweden's 1970s C74/75 spike** is worth investigating — was it a national screening / reporting initiative? Paediatric neuroblastoma in Sweden has been the subject of screening debates; this might be the incidence signature.
- Paediatric peak (band 1) + chromaffin tumour signatures would be clearer if the data were decomposed into C74 (adrenal) vs C75 (other endocrine); the aggregate makes some clean interpretations impossible.
