# Brain and central nervous system (code 48, C70–72)

## Quick take
The most important caveat to anything about brain cancer in CI5 is that registries before vol VII may have included benign tumours. Many "rises" in vol V–VI then "falls" in vol VII are almost certainly reclassification artefacts. Setting that aside, the *real* findings concern Israel, Indian registries, and a persistent East-African drop after vol X.

## What catches the eye

- **Israel Jews born in Africa / Asia women, vol V: ASR 16.58, 4.1× the world median.** Israel Jews born in Israel women also 16.05 in vol III. These are historical highs and consistent with reports of radiation-for-tinea-capitis treatment in North African immigrant cohorts (1940s–50s childhood exposure showing up decades later).
- **Inverted-U in Israel Jewish women** peaks vol III (16.05) and falls to 4.04 by vol VIII — 71 % down as the exposed cohorts pass.
- **India Trivandrum men: U-shape 11.62 → 2.44 → 2.85.** Another registry where vol VII → VIII is a scope change, not biology.
- **Multiple registries show a "vol VI-peak and decline" pattern** — USA LA Japanese men 3.06 → peak → 6.71 back to 6.71; USA NM American Indian men peaked 8.35 in vol III. Many of these sit near the benign-tumour coding boundary.
- **Rising African rates: Algeria Sétif +931 % women, +1375 % men; Uganda Kyadondo +583 % women.** From tiny baselines but upward, consistent with improving imaging access.
- **US ethnic contrasts up to 48×** — likely reflecting both real differences in incidence and differential access to MRI.
- **Sex-ratio extremes**: Yukon 10:1 male (vol VII), India Nagpur 10:1 male (vol V). Conversely Mali Bamako, Gambia, Seychelles women lead over men — small numbers but consistent direction in several African registries.
- **Bimodal age curves in large US, Belarus and Croatia samples** — childhood peak (band 1–4) + elderly peak (band 14+).
- **Peak-age 1 in Israel Jews-born-in-Africa/Asia women** — a childhood-tumour signal, possibly medulloblastoma-driven.

## Caveats
- **cancer_warning**: brain and CNS may include benign tumours before vol VII. This is the single biggest interpretation hazard in the dataset — any "vol VI → vol VII drop" must be caveated.
- Many registries changed imaging practice (CT, MRI) and coding at different times; cross-registry levels are not robust.
- India Trivandrum vol VII → VIII step is registry-scope change.

## Worth a deeper look
- **Israel Jews-from-Africa/Asia women**: a cohort paper on the declining brain-tumour burden from post-WW2 tinea-capitis irradiation. The numbers are big enough to support it.
- A systematic vol-VI-to-VII comparison across registries would reveal which countries cleaned up benign tumour coding — a methods-heavy but useful IARC internal paper.
- The African rise story is consistent across Algeria, Uganda, Barshi — likely CT-access-driven; worth documenting.
