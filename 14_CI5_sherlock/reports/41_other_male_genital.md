# Other male genital (code 41, C63)

## Quick take
C63 is a residual category (scrotum, spermatic cord, other). Very small numbers globally — most findings here reflect ascertainment-pattern differences rather than biology. The single striking outlier is the USA LA-County Korean men peak in vol V.

## What catches the eye

- **USA LA-County Korean men, vol V: ASR 3.33, 19.6× the world median.** Followed by Italy Trieste (1.76) and Uganda Gulu (1.46). This is the top spatial outlier by a wide margin — and likely reflects an over-coding of "scrotum etc." by a pathology lab rather than biology. Worth a quick check.
- **Everywhere else is near-zero.** Most registries fall to 0.00 by vol XII. The "biggest decreases" panel is dominated by registries with 0.27 → 0 transitions — more an artefact of rounding than a trend.
- **France Hérault +800 %, Korea Ulsan +420 %, Japan Saga +383 %** — small absolute rises worth investigating only if paired with the local C63 coding practice (e.g. scrotal sarcoma capture).

## Caveats
- Aggregate category with very small counts. Do NOT publish findings on "other male genital cancer" without descending to actual morphology codes.
- Historically, some C63 cases were coded under "unspecified urogenital" depending on registry — trend interpretation is essentially meaningless.

## Worth a deeper look
- None as a primary story. Useful mainly as a *data-quality* flag — registries with persistently high C63 rates may have coding issues in prostate / testis / penis.
