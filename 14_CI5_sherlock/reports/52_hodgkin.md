# Hodgkin disease (code 52, C81)

## Quick take
Hodgkin disease is the textbook "bimodal age-curve" cancer — a young-adult peak plus an older-adult peak — and CI5 captures both peaks cleanly in large US datasets. Incidence is fairly stable globally but several Mediterranean / Eastern European populations show an unusual rise, and Canadian NWT & Yukon women carry a persistent excess.

## What catches the eye

- **Canada NWT & Yukon women, vol IV: ASR 8.0, 4× the world median.** The top Hodgkin spatial outlier. Canadian Yukon men also flagged in vol VII at 7.95.
- **US Nebraska Black women vol XII: ASR 5.79, 3.5× median** — curious because the overall US Black rate is not especially elevated; worth checking the underlying subset.
- **Italian provinces (Ferrara, Benevento, Caserta) and Israel Jews-born-in-Israel women** sit at 5–6.5 ASR, 3× median. Italy's persistence of young-adult Hodgkin signals is well-documented.
- **Italy Latina men +273 %, France Doubs +321 %** (both inverted-U). Real trends not artefacts.
- **Bimodal age curves in US aggregate data**: peak at age bands 5 (20–24) and 16+ (75+) — the classic Hodgkin two-peak distribution, with a clear minimum at middle-age. CI5 visualises this fingerprint at scale (18–20 k cases).
- **Early-peak at age band 4 (15–19) in Russia St Petersburg, US Detroit, Quebec** — the "young adult" peak dominant over the older-adult peak.
- **Sex-ratio extremes**: China Zhuhai 1:6.7 female (a rare female-heavy Hodgkin), Peru Trujillo 8.6:1 male. Both unusual.
- **Singapore Malay women: +704 % (0.24 → 1.93).** The most substantial Asian rise.

## Caveats
- Hodgkin vs non-Hodgkin classification has shifted across ICD revisions; some small changes at volume boundaries reflect that.
- Pediatric Hodgkin classification changes around ICD-9/10 may affect young-age rates.

## Worth a deeper look
- The Canadian NWT Hodgkin excess is underreported — a brief Indigenous-health note could be written.
- Italy's persistent young-adult Hodgkin signal is worth re-investigating in the context of EBV epidemiology and hygiene-hypothesis data.
- Bimodal age curves at vol-XII scale would make a compelling single figure.
