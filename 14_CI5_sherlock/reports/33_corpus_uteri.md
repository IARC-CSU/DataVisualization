# Corpus uteri (code 33, C54)

## Quick take
Endometrial cancer is rising almost everywhere, consistent with the obesity-cohort story — but the more surprising finding is that **the New Zealand Pacific-peoples** female ASR has overtaken every US subgroup at 52 per 100 000, making Pacific women the world's highest endometrial-cancer population in CI5 XII. The Japanese catch-up is also extreme.

## What catches the eye

- **New Zealand Pacific-peoples women, vol XII: ASR 52.5.** Vol XI was 50.8. That's 4.5× the world median and the single largest endometrial-cancer rate in CI5 XII — above every US subgroup. Closely tied to BMI trends in Pacific populations.
- **Japan's catch-up is the steepest U-shape in the dataset.** Osaka women 1.08 → 12.59 (+1066 %); Miyagi 2.33 → 12.97 (+457 %, U). Newfoundland women 0.70 → 20.39 (+2813 %) — the highest % increase, reflecting very low vol-I ascertainment plus genuine rise.
- **Asian migrant populations in the USA still lead the historical charts.** LA-County Filipina women reached 21.6 in vol X (3× white US women at that point); SF-Bay Japanese women have been among top registries in every volume since vol III.
- **Singapore women went from near-zero to middling rates in all three ethnicities** (Chinese 4.17 → 17.07; Malay 4.76 → 17.49; Indian 3.41 → 17.52). U-shapes — genuine rise after a vol-IV dip that looks like a scope change.
- **US White vs "Asian/PI" ratios in vol IV were 13×; by vol XII only ~3–5×.** One of the clearer cases of ethnic narrowing in the dataset.
- **China Qidong: 0.45 (vol VI) → 4.38 (vol XII), +873 %.** Same rural-catching-up-to-urban pattern we see for pancreas, colon, and breast.

## Caveats
- Early-volume corpus-vs-cervix coding was sometimes blurred; some of the vol-I → II rise in Singapore reflects improving specification rather than pure biology.
- Western-Europe registries had a known corpus-coding reclassification in the 1990s — small step-changes around vol VIII should be eyed carefully.

## Worth a deeper look
- **Pacific-peoples endometrial story**: the world's top rate, yet barely discussed in global cancer literature. A focused note would land well in a journal with Pacific readership.
- Japan's U-shape matches the breast, colon, and pancreas U-shapes in the same registries — could be bundled into a single "Japanese cancer transition" figure.
- USA SF Alameda White women: ASR 47.1 in vol IV is one of the highest *early* rates for endometrial cancer; a historical note on post-war HRT + oestrogen-only era?
