# Melanoma of skin (code 24, C43)

## Quick take
Australia and New Zealand remain an order of magnitude above the rest of the world, and the gap isn't closing. The more subtle finding is the **Hawaii-White population** — in terms of melanoma they behave exactly like Queensland/NSW, not like US mainland whites. The ethnic-within-country ratios for this cancer are the largest of any site in the dataset.

## What catches the eye

- **Queensland men vol XII: ASR 68.3, 38× the world median.** Queensland women 47.5 (33×). Tasmania women 35.3, Northern Territory non-Indigenous men 45.5. The Australian "skin cancer state" story is robust and intensifying.
- **Hawaii-White men caught up to Queensland.** 3.59 (vol I) → 55.3 (vol X) → 44.2 (vol XII), a 12-fold rise in 50 years; Hawaii-White women also reached 36.9 (vol X). Same latitude + same light-skin denominator = same outcome.
- **Huge ethnic-within-country ratios.** USA White vs Hispanic ratio 276× in vol XII (men 44.2 vs 0.16); White vs Korean 274× in vol IX; White vs Black women 208× in vol VII. These are the largest ethnic gradients in CI5 for any site.
- **European catch-up is steep.** Malta men 0.6 → 8.6 (+1361 %); UK South West 1.5 → 16.0 (+995 %); Slovenia 1.6 → 17.1 (+950 %); France Somme 1.0 → 9.8 (+916 %). Women: Spain Zaragoza 0.3 → 4.2 (+1258 %); Switzerland Geneva 2.0 → 17.5 (+768 %).
- **Iceland's late female peak.** Women 2.09 (vol I) → 19.0 peak (vol IX) → 10.6 (vol XII), inverted-U. Israeli women born in Israel peaked at 29.7 in vol VII then dropped sharply to 14.6 — consistent with sun-smart + better-screening hypotheses.
- **Bimodal age curves show up in cooler regions** (Belarus, Tyrol, UK South West). The second peak sits at age band 14–17 (mid-60s), the first at the 30–40s peak. This is a clinically-reported melanoma signature (two modes) and CI5 captures it at registry scale.
- **Early female peak in UK Oxford Region vol III and USA Hawaii vol VII** — melanoma peaks at age band 8 (35–44), which is exceptionally young and fits the pre-screening era.
- **Small registry female-heavy signals in Indian registries (Karunagappally, Cachar, Tripura)** are probably acral/subungual melanoma differences but the numbers are tiny.

## Caveats
- Historical US White vs non-White ratios of 200× depend on small numerator counts in non-White sub-populations — the ratios are real but ranking them on ratio alone overweights rare denominators.
- A portion of the generalised rise reflects better biopsy/registry practices — direct comparison of vol I and vol XII should acknowledge that.

## Worth a deeper look
- **The Hawaii-White curve matches Queensland's almost exactly** — a single chart of Queensland men + Hawaii-White men across CI5 volumes would be a beautiful visual proof that latitude + skin type drive melanoma, not "Australia as a special case".
- The female inverted-U in Iceland, Israel and Switzerland (peaking vol IX–X, now falling) is consistent with public-health intervention effects and is underreported.
- The US vol-XII White-to-Hispanic ratio (276×) is likely the largest *ethnic gradient* still active for any cancer globally.
