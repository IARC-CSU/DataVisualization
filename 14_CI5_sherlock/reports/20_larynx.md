# Larynx (code 20, C32)

## Quick take
Larynx is probably the single clearest "smoking plus alcohol" site in CI5, and its trajectory over 60 years tells the global tobacco-control story registry by registry. Two surprises: the Australian Indigenous female rate leads the world, and US Black men still sit 20–30× above US Native-American men.

## What catches the eye

- **Australia Northern Territory Indigenous women in vol XII: ASR 5.20, 11× world median.** The top spatial outlier, and genuinely unusual — female larynx cancer is rare everywhere else. Smoking + kava + possibly HPV synergy.
- **Tobacco-era collapses are dramatic in ex-high-prevalence regions.** Finland men 83.3 (vol I) — well, in larynx the cleanest one is India Mumbai men 18.2 → 3.7 (−79 %), India Poona 17.4 → 3.5 (−80 %), Hong Kong 15.1 → 2.9 (−81 %).
- **India Trivandrum shows the biggest male U-shape in the site**: 78 (vol VII) → 9 (vol VIII) → 18 (vol XII). As with pancreas / rectum in the same registry, the vol VII → VIII step is likely a scope change.
- **US ethnic contrasts are enormous — and persistent.** US male Black-to-Native-American ratio was 30× in vol VIII (17.3 vs 0.6); still 22× in vol XII (8.4 vs 0.4). This is among the largest persistent ethnic gradients in CI5.
- **Spain Zaragoza men in vol VII: ASR 17.1 vs women 0.09 — a 190:1 M:F ratio.** Hungary Szabolcs-Szatmár 137:1; Spain Cuenca 111:1. These are among the highest male-to-female ratios ever recorded for any cancer site — a window into pre-1990 Mediterranean male-only smoking cultures.
- **Rising women in Spain, Norway, Algeria** (+350 to +530 %) — the late female smoking cohort catching up.
- **Qidong men 0.13 → 1.84 (+1315 %)** — as with oral cavity and liver, this rural Chinese registry is catching up to urban levels.

## Caveats
- No formal coding warning for larynx, but glottic/supraglottic split is inconsistent across registries — only rates should be compared.
- Sex-ratio outliers in Spain / Hungary with female ASR < 0.1 are small-number sensitive; the 190:1 in Zaragoza is based on fewer than 5 female cases.

## Worth a deeper look
- The Australian-Indigenous female excess deserves its own note — larynx cancer in women at this level is highly unusual globally.
- The US Black-to-Native-American ratio has been published on before, but CI5's >60-year window shows that the gap has barely closed. A historical retrospective would land.
- A "steepest Mediterranean male decline" companion piece (France, Spain, Italy) would complement the Nordic-stomach collapse piece.
