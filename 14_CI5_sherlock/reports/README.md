# CI5 sherlock — cross-site findings summary

A curated digest of the most striking temporal or spatial patterns across all 34 cancer sites in CI5 volumes I–XII, ranked by how publishable they look and how much of the story is already well-known in the literature. Each entry cross-references the per-site report. This is the reading order; the per-site reports contain the exact registry / volume / ASR values that make each claim checkable.

## A — Most publishable novel findings

These are findings that are either underreported in the literature or where CI5's 60-year window gives a uniquely compelling visual proof.

1. **The inland-China cervical cancer rise** — Jiashan County women +738 %, Qidong +273 %, Harbin Nangang +271 %, Wuhan +185 %, Zhongshan +182 %, Beijing +167 %. All rising while coastal Shanghai and the rest of East Asia fall. This is the pattern that prompted the project; a focused figure showing the coastal-fall / inland-rise duality is the core story. See [32_cervix_uteri.md](32_cervix_uteri.md).

2. **Korea's thyroid-cancer screening bubble, caught rising and falling in one dataset.** Daegu women peaked at 119.7 per 100 000 in vol XI (2008–12), Gwangju 108.8, Daejeon 108.6, Seoul 93.8. All these fell back 20–30 % by vol XII as the screening policy was re-calibrated. The cleanest public-health-intervention fingerprint in CI5. China is 5–10 years behind on the same trajectory. See [49_thyroid.md](49_thyroid.md).

3. **Hawaii-White melanoma matches Queensland exactly.** Queensland men 68.3, Hawaii-White men 44.2 — the latter up from 3.6 in vol I, a 12-fold climb. Same latitude + same skin type = same outcome; a single-registry "natural experiment" that punctures the "Australia is unique" narrative. See [24_melanoma.md](24_melanoma.md).

4. **The Zimbabwe Harare HIV-associated eye cancer inverted-U.** African women 0.42 (vol VII) → 5.76 (vol XI) → 2.89 (vol XII) — rise tracks the HIV epidemic, partial fall tracks ART rollout. Same pattern for Harare NHL men. An extraordinarily clean ART-impact visual. See [47_eye.md](47_eye.md), [53_non_hodgkin_lymphoma.md](53_non_hodgkin_lymphoma.md).

5. **The Nordic stomach-cancer collapse.** Iceland men 97.0 → 5.1 (−95 %); Finland 74.5 → 6.0 (−92 %); Norway 47.7 → 6.1 (−87 %); Denmark 40.6 → 7.1 (−83 %). One of the most complete cancer-burden disappearances ever recorded, and it would make a gorgeous four-line chart. See [11_stomach.md](11_stomach.md).

6. **Israel's gallbladder-cancer collapse.** Jewish women 11.5 → 1.4 (vol XI), a 87 % fall. Chile stays at 25–27. Two figures on one page — "high plateau" vs "disappearing excess" — tells an interesting pair of stories the literature has not unified. See [17_gallbladder.md](17_gallbladder.md).

7. **Pacific-peoples' endometrial cancer now leads the world.** NZ Pacific-peoples women vol XII ASR 52.5 — above every US subgroup. Obesity + parity story, but nobody is publishing on it. See [33_corpus_uteri.md](33_corpus_uteri.md).

8. **Hawaii-Japanese breast cancer has a uniquely *late* age peak.** Peak at band 17 (75–79), ASR 147 in vol II. Most populations peak 10 years younger. A quiet but publishable age-curve story. See [29_female_breast.md](29_female_breast.md).

9. **Singapore Indian women's "U-shape three-site combo".** Lip/oral 37.5 → 4.7 → 5.5; cervix 40.4 → 3.7 → 4.2; oesophagus 7.6 → 8.6 → 0.5. Three aerodigestive cancers tracking roughly the same curve in the same ethnic sub-population across 40 years. Suggests a single behavioural / cultural driver (betel chewing / tobacco). See [01_lip_oral_cavity.md](01_lip_oral_cavity.md), [32_cervix_uteri.md](32_cervix_uteri.md), [10_oesophagus.md](10_oesophagus.md).

10. **The African prostate-cancer rise is not a screening artefact.** Uganda Kyadondo 5.74 → 53.0 (+823 %). Rising in regions without widespread PSA. Genuine burden increase; very different story from the PSA-driven Western wave. See [39_prostate.md](39_prostate.md).

## B — Strong known stories with a fresh angle

Findings that have been written about, but where CI5's registry-level granularity sharpens the picture.

- **The Italian sinonasal male cluster** — Parma 63:1, Basilicata 52:1, Avellino 33:1, Macerata 25:1. Wood-dust / leather-dust occupational signal still alive. See [19_nose_sinuses.md](19_nose_sinuses.md).
- **The persistent US Black × prostate excess.** Detroit Black men 202.0 in vol VIII; ratio to US Native-American men 9–14× and barely narrowing. See [39_prostate.md](39_prostate.md).
- **The persistent US Black × multiple-myeloma excess.** SF Bay Black 11.64 in vol III, Nebraska Black women 12.19 in vol XII. Ratio still 12–17× in vol X. See [55_multiple_myeloma.md](55_multiple_myeloma.md).
- **Chinese oesophagus / stomach belts (Cixian, Yangcheng, Yanting, Shexian).** Up to 97× median for Cixian women (oesophagus) and 19× for Yangcheng men (stomach). The double-burden geography tells a single pickled-vegetables / nitrosamines story. See [10_oesophagus.md](10_oesophagus.md), [11_stomach.md](11_stomach.md).
- **Chile Valdivia gallbladder + testis outlier.** Two cancers, same registry at the top — unusual and worth exploring. See [17_gallbladder.md](17_gallbladder.md), [40_testis.md](40_testis.md).
- **The Japanese "U-shape across five sites".** Miyagi / Osaka breast, colon, pancreas, corpus, other-female-genital all show the same low → lower → climb curve. One "Japan's cancer transition" figure could cover all of them. See [29_female_breast.md](29_female_breast.md), [13_colon.md](13_colon.md), [18_pancreas.md](18_pancreas.md), [33_corpus_uteri.md](33_corpus_uteri.md), [36_other_female_genital.md](36_other_female_genital.md).
- **Polish men's slow lung-cancer decline.** Highest-and-most-persistent European male lung rates; a focused tobacco-control retrospective is overdue. See [21_lung.md](21_lung.md).
- **Australian Northern Territory Indigenous larynx and sinus signals.** Indigenous women laryngeal ASR 5.20 (11× median) in vol XII. See [19_nose_sinuses.md](19_nose_sinuses.md), [20_larynx.md](20_larynx.md).

## C — Data-quality flags worth raising separately

These findings are almost certainly coding/scope artefacts — but CI5 captures them so clearly that writing them up as a methodology note (for registry-quality practitioners) would be helpful.

- **India Trivandrum vol VII → VIII cliff appears in at least six sites** (pancreas, rectum, leukaemia, bladder, corpus, NHL). Strong signature of a scope change between the two volumes. A methodology note "what happens when registry scope changes" could use this.
- **France's U-shaped pancreas trends** (Isère, Tarn, Doubs, Hérault). Coordinated coding correction masquerading as a biological dip.
- **Qatar, French Polynesia, Brunei, Libya and Brazil (several registries) all have elevated "Uterus NOS" rates** — a registry coding-quality flag.
- **Brain-and-CNS vol VI → VII step**: triggered by the vol-VII reclassification of benign tumours; look out for any trend claim that straddles that boundary.
- **Finnish / Swedish "other endocrine" 1970s spike** — almost certainly neuroblastoma being captured under C74/75 and later moved.

## D — Registry / age-curve fingerprints worth a methods piece

- **UK cervix age peak at band 6 (25–34)** across all English regions — HPV-era signature.
- **Malawi Blantyre paediatric Burkitt peak at age band 2 (5–9)**, ASR 42.4 for NHL men vol X.
- **USA aggregate bimodal age curves for Hodgkin and leukaemia** at ≥ 18 000-case scale — a textbook visualisation of the "two peaks" pattern.
- **Israeli Sephardic women childhood-brain peak at age band 1** — possibly tied to post-WW2 tinea-capitis radiation cohorts.

## What's *not* in here

- All-sites aggregates (codes 62/63) were excluded by design.
- Prostate and thyroid screening effects are covered per-site; no extra cross-site piece added.
- Lung cancer's well-known story is already public; the detailed points are in [21_lung.md](21_lung.md).
