# Liver (code 16, C22)

## Quick take
Liver cancer concentrates in *three* distinct geographies with three distinct drivers — aflatoxin (Mozambique, Qidong), liver-fluke (Thailand Khon Kaen) and HBV/HCV (East Asia). Over fifty years CI5 captures all three peaks and a creeping, almost universal rise in the West, visible even in places with no obvious risk-factor history.

## What catches the eye

- **Thailand Khon Kaen is in a class of its own for fluke-driven cholangiocarcinoma.** Male ASR 90.0 (vol VI), 97.4 (vol VII), 22× the world median; women 38.3–39.0 — also 21–24×. Persistently at these levels across five volumes.
- **Qidong City (male ASR 89.9 in vol VI) is the aflatoxin twin** — same magnitude as Khon Kaen but from a very different cause. Worth showing both in one figure to highlight the "two 90s" with different aetiologies.
- **Mozambique Lourenço Marques, vol I (1956–60): male ASR 101.3, 35× the world median.** This is the classic aflatoxin paper signal, still the single highest historical reading in the series. The age curve has a peak at age band 8 (35–44), i.e. ~30 years younger than the typical liver-cancer curve — extraordinary.
- **Osaka Prefecture men: 1.81 → 46.66 inverted-U, peaking vol VII (~1990).** This is the HCV-cohort wave washing through Japan — rose for decades, now declining. Singapore Chinese women are the female mirror (0.27 → 5.51, +1941 %).
- **Many Western countries show a creeping rise from very low baselines.** Spain Navarra men 0.72 → 8.78 (+1119 %), Canada Alberta 0.60 → 7.02 (+1070 %), India Mumbai men 0.55 → 6.84 (+1144 %). Part of this is genuine (HCV, NAFLD, migration) and part is better ascertainment of metastases-vs-primary.
- **Ethnic contrast in the USA reaches 12×** (vol VII, Asian-Pacific vs non-Hispanic White men). Dominated by first-generation immigrants from HBV-endemic countries.
- **An Israel-Jews-born-in-Israel-men rise of +2640 %** from a 0.05 baseline is probably a coding artefact from vol II rather than a real cohort story — flag in any writeup.

## Caveats
- Small and near-zero baselines in vol I–II inflate some percentage rises dramatically.
- Historical liver cancer coding in CI5 included secondary liver tumours in some registries before vol VII — the "creeping rise" in the West may be partly artefactual.
- Mozambique Lourenço Marques numbers come from a single volume (I); the magnitude is credible but cannot be tracked over time.

## Worth a deeper look
- A side-by-side chart of Khon Kaen (fluke) + Mozambique / Qidong (aflatoxin) + Osaka / Singapore Chinese (HCV) + the Western creeping rise would illustrate a "four-aetiology, one-site" story elegantly.
- Early-peak age curve in Mozambique — under 40 — is striking and could anchor a piece on aflatoxin + HBV synergy.
- The Israel-male rise needs a quick sanity check against the registry.
