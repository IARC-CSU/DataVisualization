# Gallbladder etc. (code 17, C23–24)

## Quick take
Gallbladder cancer is a classic "female excess + geographic belt" story. Chile is the global hotspot and stays that way. The unusual finding is the steep *decline* in Israel (both Jews and non-Jews) — a fall that almost nobody has written about.

## What catches the eye

- **Chile remains the world capital.** Valdivia women 27.3 (vol IX), 25.1 (X), 18.9 (XI); Bío Bío 24.6 (X). All 10–15× the world median. Reassuringly stable and well documented.
- **Israel's collapse is the biggest underreported finding here.** Jewish women went 11.5 (vol II) → 1.4 (vol XI) — 87 % down. The whole-Israel line followed at 11.4 → 1.6. European-origin Israeli-Jewish women peaked at 22.8 in vol III and fell 86 % to 2.0 by vol VIII. This is on the same order as the Chilean excess — but falling.
- **USA New Mexico American-Indian women: ASR 31.4 (vol IV).** Third-highest global outlier, 9.4× median. An underappreciated population-specific hotspot.
- **Algeria Sétif: severe female-to-male skew.** Women 3.94 vs men 0.74 (vol VI) → 9.99 vs 2.09 (vol IX), a 5:1 female:male ratio. Global vol-VI median is close to 1:1 the other way. Shared by a handful of North-African / Caribbean registries.
- **Rising tide in East and South Asia.** India Chennai men 0.15 → 1.82 (+1113 %); China Qidong women 0.44 → 2.71 (+516 %); Singapore Chinese women 0.45 → 2.04 (+353 %). Not huge in absolute terms, but a consistent direction against the global fall.
- **Bimodal age curves absent; peaks sit in the usual 70+ window.**

## Caveats
- ICD reorganisation around gallbladder / extrahepatic bile duct / ampulla has moved codes between sites across ICD-8/9/10. Some of the Israel fall is likely to be real, but some may reflect reallocation of ampullary tumours to C24 → C17 boundaries; worth verifying against registry-level notes.
- Chilean registries have had strong secular ascertainment improvements — level is credible, trend should be stated cautiously.

## Worth a deeper look
- **Why is gallbladder cancer falling so fast in Israel?** Classic risk factors (gallstones, obesity, parity, Mexican-Native/Mapuche genetics) don't obviously explain it. Early cholecystectomy rates plus change in obstetric exposures is one hypothesis.
- Pairing Chile's level with Israel's fall would make a striking "high plateau vs disappearing excess" story.
- North African female excess merits an investigation into radiographic-contrast history and obstetric factors.
