# Lung (code 21, C33–34)

## Quick take
The lung-cancer trajectory in CI5 reads like the history of the cigarette itself. Men peaked and turned down almost everywhere between vol V–VIII (1980s–90s). Women are still rising *everywhere* except a handful of early-quit leaders (USA, Canada anglophone, UK). The biggest absolute outlier remains the Canadian/New-Zealand Indigenous women, not East Asia.

## What catches the eye

- **Women peak (almost) nowhere yet.** The "biggest female decreases" panel is empty. Canada Saskatchewan, Quebec, New Brunswick, Norway and others show female ASR multiplying 7–9× since vol I. Female lung cancer is the single most universally-rising indicator in the dataset.
- **Canadian Nunavut women, vol XI: ASR 97.5.** 4.8× the world median. New Zealand Māori women reached 73 (vol VII) and have stayed at 47–73 across five volumes. Canadian NWT women 72 (vol VIII). The Indigenous female lung-cancer burden is higher than almost any male burden anywhere today.
- **The male Finland inverted-U is textbook.** 83.3 (vol I) → 100.8 peak (vol III) → 28.9 (vol XII), a 65 % fall from peak. USA White San Francisco men peaked at 86.8 (vol IV) and fell to 22.5 (vol XII), a 72 % fall from peak. The US Georgia Atlanta White men peaked at 92 and fell to 32.
- **Polish regions have the world's highest never-declined male levels.** Warsaw City men 94 (vol IV), Cracow 101 (vol IV), Warsaw Rural 65 (vol VI), peaks in the mid-1970s. Decline has been slower than in most other European registries.
- **Hungary Szabolcs-Szatmár men: 17.9 → 69.6 in four volumes — a still-rising tail.** Biggest male increase in the dataset once the noisy small-N ones are filtered out.
- **US Black-vs-Native male ratio 12× in vol IV and still 11× in vol XII.** Among women, Black-vs-Native ratio peaked at 14× in vol V. Much more persistent than comparable gradients for tobacco-related cancers.
- **Extreme male:female ratios still exist in Muslim-majority registries** — Turkey Edirne 15.5:1, Belarus 14:1, Mali 19:1, Tunisia Centre 21:1. These are the *least*-converged male:female ratios for lung cancer left in the world.

## Caveats
- **cancer_warning flags this site**: ICD-7 was used for vols I–III, with some definitional discrepancies with later volumes. Don't place too much weight on the exact vol I–II levels — directionally they are fine but the magnitude should not be over-parsed.
- US bimodal "age-curves" in the scratch output reflect population-scale registries where ascertainment slightly differs — probably not a biological bimodality.

## Worth a deeper look
- A single figure showing "women reaching men's historical peak" across 6 northern registries would be striking and publishable.
- The Indigenous (Nunavut / NWT / Māori / Alaska Native) female lung-cancer excess is in need of a dedicated review piece.
- Polish men have declined slowest in Europe — a focused analysis against smoking-prevalence and tobacco-control policy timing would inform ongoing debate.
