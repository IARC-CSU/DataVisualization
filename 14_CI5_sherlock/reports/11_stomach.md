# Stomach (code 11, C16)

## Quick take
Stomach cancer has been falling globally for 50 years — the story of a disease chased down by refrigeration, falling *H. pylori* prevalence and better food hygiene. CI5 captures the decline with textbook clarity, but also the hold-out belts in East Asia and inland China.

## What catches the eye

- **The Nordic collapse is near-total.** Iceland men 97.0 → 5.1 (−95 %), Finland 74.5 → 6.0 (−92 %), Norway 47.7 → 6.1 (−87 %), Denmark 40.6 → 7.1 (−83 %). Women in the same countries dropped 86–92 %. Together these constitute one of the most complete disappearances of a cancer burden ever recorded.
- **The Chinese belt matches the oesophagus belt — same counties.** Yangcheng males 168.9 (vol X), Shexian 151.9 (vol XI), Yanting 144.6 (vol XI), Cixian 118.0 (vol XI). 14–19× the world median. Three of these counties also top the oesophagus list — same rural diet signal (pickled vegetables, nitrosamines) hitting both sites.
- **Hawaii Japanese shows the migrant inversion classic.** Inverted-U: 61.2 (vol I, Japan-imported rates) → peak 66.1 (vol II) → 7.7 (vol XII). Their origin rates in Japan are still falling too, but the Hawaii curve falls *faster* — environment dominates genetics.
- **Ugandan rise.** Kyadondo men 2.43 → 6.06 (+149 %) and women 1.08 → 4.55 (+321 %). Small absolute numbers, but the direction is counter to the global trend and worth checking subsite (cardia vs non-cardia).
- **US ethnic spread still 12×.** Within the USA vol X, male stomach ASR ranges 3.3 (Native American) to 39.4 (Black); women 1.4 to 17.6. Almost entirely driven by Korean- and Vietnamese-heritage populations at the top.

## Caveats
- None of these findings rely on a coding break.
- The Iceland/Finland baselines in vol I (1955–1965) may include some oesophageal cardia — but the magnitude is large enough that the story survives.

## Worth a deeper look
- A "global Nordic-collapse" figure comparing Iceland / Finland / Norway / Denmark on a single chart would be striking.
- Why is the decline slower (or absent) in Chinese rural registries? Pickled-food penetration, *H. pylori* prevalence, refrigeration access — a geographic story waiting to be told.
- Uganda: is the rise driven by cardia adenocarcinoma (obesity / reflux signal) or non-cardia (*H. pylori*)?
