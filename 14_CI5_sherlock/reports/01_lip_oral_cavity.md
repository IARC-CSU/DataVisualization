# Lip, oral cavity (code 1, C00–C14)

## Quick take
Two very different stories sit under the same label. The "classic" Western lip cancer — high in agricultural outdoor populations (Canada, Malta, Italy) — is collapsing, while intra-oral cancer in South Asia and East China is either exploding or simply running at astonishing rates.

## What catches the eye

- **Singapore Indian women follow a perfect U-shape.** Vol III → X: 37.5 → 4.7 per 100 000, then rebound to 5.5 in vol XII. Same cohort, same registry, 85 % fall and partial recovery — exactly the kind of pattern the Shanghai cervix plot highlighted. The ASR in vol III (37.5) is 8.8× the global female median — tied for top spatial outlier in the dataset.
- **Canadian lip cancer disappearing.** Newfoundland men fell 38.3 → 11.8 (−69 %), Saskatchewan 36.7 → 9.3 (−75 %), New Brunswick 34.7 → 11.3 (−68 %), all through a characteristic U (high-high-drop-bottom-small rebound). Plausibly fewer outdoor fishermen/farmers + UV protection — the lip vermilion component.
- **Inland-China intra-oral emergence.** Jiashan County females +738 %, Qidong +274 %, Harbin +271 %, Wuhan +185 % since vol VIII. Even Shanghai rose in women while falling in men — unusual sex divergence.
- **India & Pakistan dominate absolute levels.** Kamrup Urban (Assam) males 59.5, Meghalaya 52.3, Pakistan South Karachi women 23.8 — all 5–6× the world median. Betel quid / gutkha belt.
- **USA ethnic gaps are enormous.** In vol IV–V the US male top-to-bottom ratio across ethnicities was 27×; US vol V women had a 21× spread. The highest male subgroup was SF-Bay Black (ethnic 30), the lowest was Hispanic (42).
- **Bimodal age curves in Algeria and Jiashan** — the female curve has a visible low-age hump before the usual 60+ peak, which is unusual for oral cancer.

## Caveats
- None of the main findings ride on coding breaks for this site.
- Uganda Kyadondo's +1548 % male change must be read with the vol-VII pop_warning — likely reflects population-denominator change.
- US ethnic subgroup spreads before vol VI should be read cautiously (small subgroup pops, early vol).

## Worth a deeper look
- The Singapore-Indian U: is the rebound real, and driven by chewing-tobacco or by oral-HPV? Registry-level subsite split would tell.
- Can the inland-China rises be reconciled with the falls in coastal Shanghai men? A rural-to-urban dietary / tobacco story could fit.
- Document the Newfoundland/Saskatchewan/Malta lip collapse as a coherent Western-lip-vermilion story.
