# Penis (code 38, C60)

## Quick take
Penile cancer is a developing-country, low-circumcision cancer that has been falling almost everywhere — but *not* in Switzerland and parts of Eastern Europe, where it is creeping up. Brazil Recife, Jamaica Kingston, and Uganda are the persistent global hotspots.

## What catches the eye

- **Brazil Recife men, vol III: ASR 9.54 and vol V: 7.94** — roughly 10–11× the world median. One of the most striking historical South American rates.
- **Jamaica Kingston, vol I: 10.84, then falling to 1.10 by vol XI** — a 90 % decline, among the cleanest falls for any cancer in the Caribbean.
- **Uganda Gulu and Zimbabwe Bulawayo African men sit above 5 in vol XII** — African penile cancer remains ~10× world median while Latin American registries have largely converged to global levels.
- **Switzerland, Poland and a handful of Japanese prefectures are going against the grain.** Valais +292 %, Graubünden 0.38 → 1.12, Poland Warsaw Rural +162 %, Japan Saga and Yamagata rising from 0.13–0.14 baselines. Small numbers but unexpected.
- **USA Missouri Black men: +369 % (0.13 → 0.61).** The only US Black sub-population with a visible rise.
- **Ethnic contrast inside Singapore is 11:1 in vol III** — Indian men highest, Chinese lowest. By vol XII the ratio has narrowed to ~3:1.
- **China Shanghai and Hong Kong men: clean U-shapes** (Shanghai 1.58 → 0 → 0.31, Hong Kong 1.95 → 0 → 0.37). Both reflect improving hygiene / early circumcision increase during urban transition.

## Caveats
- Small counts everywhere — treat all % changes cautiously.
- CI5's penis code (C60) includes both invasive SCC and in-situ; some of the Swiss rise may reflect better capture of Tis.

## Worth a deeper look
- The Swiss + Polish + Japanese "creeping rise" is plausible enough (HPV + demographic shifts) to warrant a regional look. It would also tie neatly to the cervix rise in inland China — a HPV-cluster paper for penile + cervix could work.
- Revisit Brazil Recife's 1970s–80s readings with an ethnographic lens: still one of the highest per-capita historical rates for any penile cancer population.
