# Small intestine (code 12, C17)

## Quick take
A rare cancer (median ASR around 1) but one of the clearest *rising* trends in the dataset — virtually every high-income registry is going up, and the rise is especially steep in Black populations in the USA.

## What catches the eye

- **Almost no registry is falling.** The "biggest decreases" panel is empty for both sexes — unusual in CI5.
- **US Black populations are climbing fast.** Louisiana/New Orleans Black men 0.32 → 2.98 (+831 %, inverted-U shape peaking in vol XI); Connecticut Black men +855 %; Louisiana/New Orleans Black women 0.37 → 3.75 (+913 %).
- **Chinese registries rising from near-zero.** Xiangfu District, Kaifeng City, reaches 10.5 in men vol XII — that's 8.7× the world male median and easily the highest ASR in the dataset for this site. Jiashan County women show a clean inverted-U peaking at 1.22 in vol XI.
- **New Zealand Māori women have tripled.** 0.28 (vol II) → 2.08 (vol XII), inverted-U peaking in vol X.
- **Strong male-heavy skew almost everywhere.** Poland Cracow 19:1, Spain Albacete 14:1, France Tarn 12:1. Vol-XII world median M:F is only ~1.5. Why this sex gap is so large is worth interrogating — small-bowel carcinoids are thought to be roughly equal across sexes.
- **Ethnic spread inside the USA is 13×.** Black populations at the top, Native American and Korean immigrant populations at the bottom.

## Caveats
- Small absolute numbers; any single-registry trend should be read with confidence intervals.
- Coding drift between carcinoid and adenocarcinoma over the CI5 era is a known issue — some of the rise may reflect better counting of neuroendocrine tumours (especially in registries with strong pathology). Worth flagging explicitly in any writeup.

## Worth a deeper look
- The US Black steep rise: obesity/diet, or better histological capture of carcinoids? Stratifying by morphology in SEER-quality registries would help.
- Xiangfu (Kaifeng) being the global top outlier is surprising and worth a quick phone call to the registry. Could be a coding issue mixing duodenum with stomach/pancreas.
- The universally male-heavy sex ratio in Europe deserves an explanation — Crohn's-related small-bowel adenocarcinoma?
