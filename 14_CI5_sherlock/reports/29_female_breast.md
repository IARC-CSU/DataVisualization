# Female breast (code 29, C50)

## Quick take
Breast cancer rose everywhere and in volume XII still rises almost everywhere — the cleanest universally-rising cancer of the twelve volumes. The most striking individual findings are the U-shapes in Japanese and Hawaii-Japanese women (where the fall around vol II–III was real, not artefact) and the world-record absolute rate in US-LA-County Filipino women (100.0).

## What catches the eye

- **The biggest absolute rate in the whole breast dataset: LA-County Filipina women, vol X, ASR 100.0.** Still 83.2 in vol XII after some decline. This beats every US White and US Black subgroup.
- **Clean U-shape in Japan and migrant Japanese women.** Miyagi Prefecture 15.3 (vol I) → 12.8 (vol II) → 74.6 (vol XII); Nagasaki 26.3 → 20.3 (vol V) → 56.4. Hawaii Filipina 25.0 (vol II) → 24.7 (vol III) → 85.9 (vol XII). All "U" because the first volume under-ascertained then the catch-up was steep.
- **Singapore Chinese +785 % and Algeria Sétif +772 %** — the catch-up is not limited to East-Asian migrants.
- **Extreme bimodal age curves in Hawaii Japanese women.** Peak shifts to age band 17 (75–79) rather than the usual 13–14 (60–69). In vol II the peak was 147 per 100 000 at 75–79. This is highly unusual — most populations have a late-60s peak or earlier. Hawaii Japanese women have a *later* peak than anyone else.
- **India Karunagappally women: early peak at age 12 (55–59).** Notable because most breast-cancer peaks sit 10 years older; the premenopausal / perimenopausal hump in South Asian women has been described before but CI5 shows it here clearly with a large case count.
- **US ethnic contrast in vol V: 6.7× spread** (White 93.9 vs Korean 14.1). By vol XII the gap has closed to roughly 2.5× — but Filipina women *overshot* the US White rate to reach the top of the distribution. That inversion deserves its own note.
- **Male breast cancer: +4800 % in Latvia (0.01 → 0.49 inverted-U).** The largest percentage change in the dataset but from a vanishing baseline — not meaningful on its own but consistent with a broad male-breast rise that merits attention.
- **Uganda Kyadondo: 11.39 (vol I) → 32.93 (vol X) → 32.0 (vol XII).** Breast cancer in East Africa tripled in 60 years — the African epidemic story.

## Caveats
- Breast screening roll-outs in different decades mean that a rising curve during vol VIII–X often captures a prevalence pool being worked through, not pure underlying incidence.
- Hawaii Japanese late-peak numbers are small-N at band 17; sanity-check with larger aggregated data before making a story of it.

## Worth a deeper look
- **The LA-County Filipina rate (100.0) is the highest breast-cancer ASR in CI5** — it beats every US White registry. Worth asking: acculturation effect, reproductive patterns, or registry differences?
- The Japanese / migrant-Japanese U-shape: compare Miyagi with Hawaii Japanese across volumes to show the genetics-vs-environment separation.
- African rise: Uganda Kyadondo, Algeria Sétif, Israel Arabs all share the same trajectory (+500 % to +770 %). A coordinated "breast cancer goes continental" piece writes itself.
