# Testis (code 40, C62)

## Quick take
Testis is the cleanest generational-rise story in CI5 — rates roughly triple in virtually every high-income country between vol I and vol XII. The "Swiss–Alpine belt" and Chile Valdivia sit at the top in vol XII, and US Hawaiian and Asian-migrant sub-populations show dramatic late catch-ups.

## What catches the eye

- **Swiss and Austrian alpine registries dominate vol XII.** Graubünden+Glarus 14.4, Berne 12.6, Lucerne 12.0, Aargau 11.4, Carinthia 11.3. The "Alpine testis belt" is a known feature; CI5 XII confirms it has strengthened further.
- **Chile Valdivia men, vol XII: ASR 15.5 — the world's highest.** 6.4× the world median. This is counter-intuitive (testis is normally a high-income-country cancer) and worth a closer look. Possibly reflects high early-life undescended-testis prevalence + catching-up through registry improvement.
- **US Puerto Rico: 0.45 (vol I) → 4.94 (vol XII), +998 %.** Largest increase in the set.
- **US Hawaii Filipino and Chinese men: late U-shape.** Filipino 0.23 → 3.49 peak vol XI → 1.85; Chinese 0.74 → 7.05 peak vol VI then fall. Both have a hawk-like rise then drop — probably birth-cohort exhaustion.
- **Huge ethnic contrast within USA.** In vol IV, White vs Black ratio 27:1 (5.40 vs 0.20); vol XII Other-White vs Black 26:1 (8.04 vs 0.31). One of the most *widening* ethnic gradients in CI5 — the rest of the world rose but US Black testis cancer didn't.
- **The adolescent peak (age band 5 = 20–24) is in every registry** — textbook.
- **China Taiwan vol VIII shows an early-peak at age band 1 (0–4):** probably yolk-sac tumours of infancy, a known pattern in East Asian cohorts.

## Caveats
- Male-only cancer, no cancer_warning.
- The generational rise is real but some of it reflects earlier detection of subclinical seminoma; historical registries of the 1960s almost certainly under-counted.

## Worth a deeper look
- The Chile Valdivia outlier is worth investigating — the alpine Latin-American testis-cancer literature is thin.
- The widening US White-vs-Black gap is an underreported disparity.
- Map the Swiss Alpine cluster against the Austrian one and test for genuine biological continuity (altitude, oxidative-stress hypotheses exist but are speculative).
