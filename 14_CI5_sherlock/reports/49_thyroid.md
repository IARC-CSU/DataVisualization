# Thyroid (code 49, C73)

## Quick take
Thyroid is the most dramatic cancer-trend story in CI5, period. **South Korea** stands alone: registry-level female ASR around 120 per 100 000 in vol XI — nearly 10× the world median and higher than any other registry-sex-cancer combination in the dataset. This is the textbook case of screening-driven over-diagnosis and CI5 captures it with unmatched clarity.

## What catches the eye

- **South Korea: Daegu women, vol XI, ASR 119.7. Gwangju 108.8. Daejeon 108.6. Ulsan 96.6. Seoul 93.8.** Vol XI (2008–2012) was the screening-bubble peak. By vol XII the rates had fallen back (Daegu 87.3, Seoul 64.7) as the over-screening was reined in. Inverted-U shapes in virtually every Korean city. This is the single cleanest public-health intervention effect in CI5.
- **Chinese cities are rising fast — not yet past peak.** Jiashan County women +4432 % (1.18 → 53.48); Harbin Nangang +2665 %; Wuhan +1834 %; Qidong (U-shape) 0.69 → 12.19. Men in Jiashan +2875 %, Beijing +1888 %, Wuhan +1575 %. The Chinese story looks exactly like the Korean one did at vol X — rise still ongoing.
- **Italy Latina women: 1.55 → 31.83 peak → 29.66** — a European microcosm of the Korean pattern. Italy generally is a high-and-rising thyroid country.
- **Sex ratio: thyroid is one of the few cancers where women outweigh men 3–5 to 1 almost everywhere.** The strength of the imbalance is a thyroid-cancer fingerprint.
- **Early peak (age band 6–7, ~25–35 yrs) in many populations with high screening uptake.** USA Iowa and Wisconsin women peak at 25–34, as do Trivandrum, several Chinese cities. This is papillary thyroid cancer being found incidentally in young women during other imaging.
- **US ethnic contrasts: 15× between Asian-Pacific and Native American groups.**
- **Hawaii Chinese women vol II: ASR 22.86, 7.6× the world median of 1970.** An early, pre-CT outlier that has not received much attention.
- **Bimodal age curves in several Western registries** (LA Non-Hispanic White, Detroit White, Israel Jews). Two-peak distributions: one young, one old — reflects the papillary-vs-follicular histology split.

## Caveats
- Most of the rise globally is *incidentalomas* — sub-clinical papillary cancers detected during ultrasound for unrelated reasons. Mortality from thyroid cancer has barely moved. Be explicit in any writeup.
- No formal CI5 cancer_warning but the histology mix (papillary / follicular / medullary / anaplastic) is not stated; the screening signal is overwhelmingly papillary.

## Worth a deeper look
- **The Korean national-screening program's rise and fall is the single most striking epidemiological intervention signal in CI5.** A standalone chart of Daegu / Gwangju / Daejeon / Ulsan / Seoul inverted-U would be the strongest plot the project can produce.
- **China is on the same trajectory 5–10 years behind Korea** — a prospective comparison could warn policy makers that the Chinese inverted-U peak is still ahead.
- The young-age peak (25–35) for papillary thyroid cancer is globally consistent; documenting the age-shift across vols I–XII tells the over-diagnosis story chronologically.
