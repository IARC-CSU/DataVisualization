# Eye (code 47, C69)

## Quick take
Eye cancer is almost entirely uveal melanoma in the West and retinoblastoma in the Global South. The striking CI5 finding is the East-African signal — Malawi Blantyre, Zimbabwe Harare, Bulawayo — where eye-cancer ASRs are 10–20× the world median, almost certainly driven by HIV-related ocular surface squamous neoplasia (OSSN), not melanoma.

## What catches the eye

- **Malawi Blantyre women, vol X: ASR 9.81, 20× the world median.** By far the top spatial outlier. Malawi men vol X 7.43 (13×); Zimbabwe Harare women vol XI 5.76 (13×); Zimbabwe Bulawayo men vol XII 5.20 (13×). This is the OSSN (HIV-associated ocular surface squamous neoplasia) epidemic, visible in CI5.
- **Zimbabwe Harare African women inverted-U**: 0.42 (vol VII) → 5.76 (vol XI) → 2.89 (vol XII). The rise phase matches the HIV epidemic, the fall matches ART roll-out. Astonishingly clean.
- **Germany Saarland women: +1200 %** (0.05 → 0.82 → 0.65). Italy Genova +733 %, Switzerland Geneva +618 %. Part of a western "rising incidental detection" pattern as ophthalmological services tighten.
- **Bimodal age curves in most large US female populations** — peak in older ages but a secondary bump in middle age. This is the mix of retinoblastoma (early band) and uveal melanoma (older) + OSSN, though OSSN dominates the adult low-age signal.
- **Japanese / Korean women: early-age peak at band 1 (0–4).** Retinoblastoma-dominant population distribution. India Tamil Nadu and USA Black women also have a band-1 peak, consistent with retinoblastoma.
- **Extraordinary sex ratios** — Japan Hiroshima 35:1 male, Italy Modena 36:1 male, Spain Cuenca 19:1 male in one direction, and Spain Ciudad Real 1:18 female, Japan Akita 1:19 female. These small-number outliers reflect single-case swings.

## Caveats
- Very small numbers for most registries. The African numbers are robust because they are large relative to the small Western denominators.
- Eye cancer aggregates retinoblastoma, uveal melanoma, OSSN, orbital lymphoma — without histology split, cross-registry comparison is perilous.

## Worth a deeper look
- **The HIV-OSSN signal in Malawi / Zimbabwe is the most publishable finding on this site.** Paired with HIV prevalence / ART timelines, the inverted-U in Harare African women could be a compact visual proof of ART impact on this cancer.
- The adult band-1 peak in Japanese / Korean women should be decomposed into retinoblastoma age breakdown — a clean paper on "retinoblastoma age distribution over the CI5 era" could come out of this.
